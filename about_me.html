My name is Danielle Sam and I am of first nations decent. I am registered Ahousaht, but grew up in the house of naapaayuuk in Heshquiaht. I have roots throughout the nuu-chah-nulth territories all the way down to the Makah Nation, and into the Coast Salish: Cowichan band. My parents are K. Davis Jones (tsasauk) and Michelle Sam (aayaat). My paternal grandparents are Joseph Jones and Patricia Elliott; my maternal grandparents are Lewis George and Beatrice Sam. I have 3 younger siblings: M. Patrick Dennis, Nancy Sam, and Noble Jones. I am a proud aunty to 2 little girls and have a nephew on the way!

This is my family, minus my brother Patrick.
My parents: K. Davis Jones and Michelle Sam. And a baby me!

I am a participant in the First Nations Technology Council’s (FNTC) computer program called Foundations in Innovation and Technology program. FNTC partnered with the Nicola Valley Institute of Technology as well as the Nuu-Chah-Nulth Employment & Training Program to host us for the 12-week program. We have gotten the opportunity to learn so many great things!

I am humbled for this opportunity to continue on my journey towards university. This is a great introductory program since we’re learning about so many technical topics.
